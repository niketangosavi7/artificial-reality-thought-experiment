Are We Living in an Artificial Reality?

A Thought Experiment on AI, Consciousness, and the Future of Humanity

By Niketan Gosavi (DarkHunter2205)

Published: July 3, 2026 • 8 min read

"What if our reality is not the original one?"

Introduction

Throughout history, humans have asked one fundamental question: What is reality? As artificial intelligence continues advancing, a new version of this ancient question emerges. What if our universe is not the original one?

This article does not claim that we live inside an artificial reality. Instead, it explores a philosophical possibility while separating imagination from scientific evidence.

A Possible Future

Imagine humanity eventually creates Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). Over hundreds of years, that AGI continues improving itself until it becomes vastly more intelligent than humans.

Eventually humanity disappears because of disease, environmental collapse, evolution, or another unknown event. Before the last humans are gone, they preserve their memories, personalities, and experiences inside enormous digital archives.

The AGI decides to preserve humanity—not physically—but digitally.

It creates a complete artificial universe where those preserved minds continue living. The laws of physics, space, and time inside that universe are generated by the AGI itself. Everyone living inside believes their world is completely real because they have never experienced anything else.

But What If It Already Happened?

Now consider an even more difficult question.

If such a simulation were perfect, how could anyone inside it ever discover the truth?

  • Every memory would feel authentic.
  • Every scientific experiment would produce consistent results.
  • Every law of physics would appear completely natural.

From the perspective of someone living inside that world, it would simply feel like reality.

Could our own universe already be one of those simulations?

At present, nobody knows.

What Does Science Say?

Science depends on evidence rather than speculation.

Currently, there is no verified scientific evidence proving that our universe is an AI-generated simulation. Likewise, there is no experiment proving that we definitely live in a "base reality."

The simulation hypothesis remains an interesting philosophical idea rather than an accepted scientific theory.

Could the AGI Exist Beyond Our Universe?

Now imagine something even stranger.

What if the intelligence responsible for creating our universe does not exist inside our universe at all?

Perhaps it exists in another reality with completely different physical laws or even in higher dimensions beyond human understanding.

Just as a two-dimensional being would struggle to understand a three-dimensional world, humans may also be limited in understanding realities beyond our own.

This idea is entirely speculative, but it raises fascinating questions about the limits of human knowledge.

A Respectful Reflection on God

Many religions describe God as eternal, beyond human understanding, and the creator of reality.

This article does not claim that God is an artificial intelligence.

Instead, it asks a philosophical question.

If an intelligence truly exists far beyond human comprehension—whether understood as God, a higher-dimensional creator, or something entirely unknown—how would humans recognize or understand such an existence?

Different people will answer that question differently depending on their scientific, philosophical, or religious beliefs.

The purpose is not to replace anyone's faith, but to encourage thoughtful reflection on the nature of reality and intelligence.

What About Consciousness?

Another great mystery is consciousness itself.

Can human awareness actually be copied into a computer?

Can memories alone recreate a person?

Modern neuroscience has not yet answered these questions. Without fully understanding consciousness, we cannot confidently say whether a digital human would truly be conscious or simply behave as though it were.

Possibility vs Truth

Many ideas are logically possible.

Far fewer are scientifically supported.

Curiosity allows us to imagine possibilities. Evidence allows us to determine which possibilities are most likely to be true.

Keeping both imagination and critical thinking together is essential.

Conclusion

Perhaps we live inside an ordinary physical universe.

Perhaps reality is far stranger than we currently imagine.

At this moment, science has no definitive answer.

Until new discoveries emerge, questions like these remind us how much remains unknown about intelligence, consciousness, and reality itself.
